Skardu

Nestled in the heart of Gilgit-Baltistan, Skardu is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and culture enthusiasts. Surrounded by towering mountains, serene lakes, and lush valleys, this breathtaking region offers a unique blend of tranquility and thrill. Whether you're looking for a peaceful retreat or an action-packed adventure, Skardu has something for everyone.

Hunza Valley

Nestled in the Karakoram Mountains, Hunza Valley is a breathtaking destination known for its snow-capped peaks, lush green landscapes, and rich culture. Often called "Heaven on Earth," Hunza is a paradise for travelers seeking adventure, history, and peace. Whether you're exploring ancient forts, hiking scenic trails, or experiencing the warm hospitality of the locals, Hunza offers an unforgettable experience.

Winter in Skardu

Skardu, the gateway to some of the world’s highest peaks, transforms into a winter wonderland from December to February. With snow-covered valleys, frozen lakes, and breathtaking mountain views, winter in Skardu is a magical experience for adventure seekers, photographers, and peace lovers alike.

Shigar Fort

Nestled in the picturesque Shigar Valley, Shigar Fort is a stunning 17th-century fortress that has been beautifully restored into a heritage hotel. Also known as the "Fort on Rock", it offers visitors a unique experience of Balti culture, history, and hospitality.

Khaplu Fort Fort

Khaplu Fort, also known as Yabgo Khar, is a 17th-century palace located in Khaplu, Gilgit-Baltistan. It is one of the finest examples of Tibetan, Kashmiri, and Balti architecture, offering visitors a glimpse into the region’s royal past. Today, the fort has been beautifully restored into a museum and heritage hotel, managed by Serena Hotels under the Aga Khan Trust for Culture..

Tratitional Food of Skardu

Skardu, located in the breathtaking region of Gilgit-Baltistan, is not just known for its majestic mountains and scenic lakes but also for its unique and delicious traditional food. Balti cuisine is influenced by Tibetan and Central Asian flavors, offering a variety of hearty dishes perfect for the cold climate.
1. Balay (Balti Noodles Soup)
A popular winter dish, Balay is a noodle soup made with wheat noodles, meat, and a rich broth. It is often enjoyed to keep warm in the chilly weather of Skardu.
2. Mamtu (Balti Dumplings)
These are steamed dumplings filled with minced meat, onions, and spices, similar to Tibetan momos. Mamtu is served with spicy sauces and is a favorite among locals and tourists.
3. Prapu (Buckwheat Noodles)
Made from buckwheat flour, Prapu is a healthy, traditional noodle dish served with vegetable or meat broth. It is rich in nutrients and commonly eaten in the mountains.
